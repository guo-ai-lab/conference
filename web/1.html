Call for Papers:
Evolutionary Computation for Safety: Data, Methods and Applications

IEEE Congress on Evolutionary Computation, June 8 - 12, 2025, Hangzhou, China

Submission Deadline: May 1, 2025

Aim & Scope:

The aim of this special session is to explore innovative applications of evolutionary computation in safety engineering and safety-critical systems. The session seeks to advance both theoretical understanding and practical applications of evolutionary algorithms in safety monitoring, prediction, and optimization. We welcome contributions that demonstrate novel approaches to enhancing safety through evolutionary computation across various domains.

This special session investigates the integration of evolutionary computation with safety engineering to address key challenges in system safety, monitoring, and optimization. We focus on both methodological innovations and practical implementations, inviting contributions that showcase the effectiveness of evolutionary methods in improving safety across different application scenarios.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

1. Safety System Design and Optimization:

  • Evolutionary optimization of safety system architecture
  • Adaptive safety parameter tuning
  • Multi-objective safety design optimization
  • Reliability enhancement using evolutionary algorithms

    • 2. Safety Monitoring and Prediction:

  • Time series analysis for safety monitoring
  • Real-time safety pattern recognition
  • Predictive maintenance optimization
  • Early warning system design

    • 3. Safety Data Analytics:

  • Evolutionary feature extraction for safety data
  • Pattern mining in accident/incident databases
  • Anomaly detection in safety-critical systems
  • Handling uncertainty in safety assessment

    • 4. Intelligent Safety Control:

  • Adaptive safety control strategies
  • Emergency response optimization
  • Dynamic safety boundary identification
  • Robust safety decision making

    • 5. Real-World Applications:

  • Industrial process safety
  • Transportation safety systems
  • Construction site safety
  • Healthcare safety
  • Environmental safety
  • Nuclear plant safety
  • Aviation safety

    • This special session invites contributions that present innovative approaches using evolutionary computation for safety enhancement. We encourage submissions that explore new methodologies, provide comprehensive evaluations, and demonstrate practical implementations in safety-critical environments.
